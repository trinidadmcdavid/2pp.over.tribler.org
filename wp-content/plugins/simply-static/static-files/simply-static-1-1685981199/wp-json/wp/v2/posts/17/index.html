Saudi Arabia has announced to reduce oil production by another 1 million barrels per day. It is believed that such a decision has been taken to increase the price of oil in the world market by reducing the supply.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The announcement was made after a meeting between the 13-nation Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC) and its 10 allies led by Saudi Arabia. Russia led the Allies. OPEC and 10 allied countries are called OPEC Plus.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Saudi Arabia’s Energy Minister Prince Abdulaziz bin Salman told reporters that the decision to cut production was made for the month of July. Its duration may be extended.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The decision to cut ‘voluntary’ production was announced after representatives of OPEC plus countries took part in several hours of talks in the Austrian capital Vienna.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

A source involved in this discussion says that there has been a discussion about reducing production by 1 million barrels per day.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

About 60 percent of the world’s total oil is produced in OPEC plus countries. Oil-producing nations have struggled to cope with falling oil prices and volatile markets since Russia’s invasion of Ukraine began in February 2022.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Last April, several OPEC Plus members agreed to voluntarily cut production by more than 1 million barrels a day. Although this sudden action reduced the price of oil, it was not sustainable for long.
Oil prices fell nearly 10 percent after announcing production cuts in April. Brent crude oil prices fell to $70 per barrel. This is the first time since December 2021 that the price of Brent crude has fallen so much.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
